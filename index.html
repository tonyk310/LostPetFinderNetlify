LostPetFinderLA provides FREE tips for Lost Pet Owners.

DON'T PANIC

The AKC reports that 1-in-3 pets will go missing in their lifetimes. The most important thing for you and your family is to remain calm in this stressful situation. Every moment counts!

PREPARATIONS

Take multiple photos of your pet clearly showing distinguishing features including: fur color, size & markings. Make sure your pet has a secured collar with the pets name and owners phone numbers on the tags. has chip

GATHER SUPPLIES

Gather together your pets favorite toys, snacks and anything else your pet enjoys and place them outside of your front door. If your pet returns it will be distracted. Prepare a leash, a fully charged cell phone and a pen and notepad to write notes with.

IMMEDIATELY AFTER ESCAPE

Call out loudly to your pet. Do not yell or shout, your tone of voice is very important to a frightened animal. Try to limit panic and anxiety. Use your pets favorite squeaky toy to make noise and call out to your pet.

THINKING LIKE YOUR PET

Begin to search your immediate area. Think of areas in your neighborhood that your pet is familiar with and is likely to go. Daily walking paths, favorite fire hydrants, other dogs or cats in the neighborhood they may be familiar with.

SEARCH YOUR IMMEDIATE AREA

As you expand your search look for neighbors, joggers or other pet owners who may be sympathetic and ask them to keep a look out for your pet. Write down your contact info for them on your notepad. Make sure to let them know there is a reward.

FEW HOURS OR DAYS SINCE ESCAPE

Now is the time to start thinking bigger picture. Begin to spread the word to the community at large. Contact local Police Departments and Animal control departments. Contact local Veterinarians and animal shelters.

LOST PET POSTERS

Begin to construct your lost pet poster. make sure to include a clear descriptive photo of your pet. The pets name, color, marking, owners contact information.
